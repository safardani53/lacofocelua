Things to Consider Before Buying a Puffer Jacket

Materials

When considering a puffer jacket, the most important aspect is the material because that’s what keeps you warm. “If you're looking for warmth and cozy comfort, a functional and fashionable puffer jacket should be made of down,” says Volpe. “Premium down is more luxurious and has the best insulation-to-weight ratio of any material. Not only is it super warm, but it's also easy to fold down for your next outdoor adventure.”

Deatherage agrees. “I find the weight of a puffer to be crucial,” he says. “If you are someone who likes to layer, look for a lightweight puffer with a polyfill. If you want a piece that’s more substantial in colder climates I look for a down fill and a lining that provide that extra warmth.” Nylon and polyester are good lining materials that provide substantial warmth.

Volpe adds that you can play around with different material choices depending on your needs and preferences. “I also go for more unique material choices such as leather to make a statement,” she says. “However, if you're the outdoorsy type and you're seeking a puffer jacket for cold weather conditions like a mountain hike, you're going to want to size up for layering and likely purchase synthetic materials so the jacket doesn't lose insulation from water damage in the snow.”

Silhouette

The silhouette or cut of a puffer jacket will determine how it fits. Some are a little cinched, which can make for a snug fit when layering with sweaters and other garments. Others are bulkier and not as easily packable. “When purchasing a puffer jacket there are so many things to consider,” says Volpe. “For me personally, I purchase a puffer jacket for fashion (and a little bit for warmth) versus function. Due to this, I look for a fun cropped puffer [like the Lululemon Wunder Puff Cropped Jacket] that's a bit oversized for layering a fun turtleneck or printed sweater underneath.”

Pricing

The other consideration that can’t be overlooked is pricing. Puffer jackets — like most outwear — can run the gambit in cost, and if you’re not careful, you can overspend. We’ve worked hard to find selects for every budget but have made sure that the quality is top tier, no matter the price. Some picks are expensive, like the Alo Yoga Ice Breaker Puffer Jacket, but they offer a high quality and value that matches the price. That being said, our lower-budget picks, like the Uaneo Casual Padded Puffer, Dina Weis Shiny Padded Puffer Jacket, and Tsla Packable Accent Puffer Jacket, can also stand the test of time and provide functional fashion to get you through the winter.

Frequently Asked Questions

How should a puffer jacket fit? 

The fit of a puffer jacket comes down to sizing and silhouette, as well as the clothes you’re wearing underneath. “Sizing is always an important part of choosing your puffer,” says Deatherage. “Layering is great for those on the go, especially in the winter months. If you want to use a puffer as an under layer I suggest a more snug size as an appropriate fit. If you want your puffer to be an outer layer, maybe size up to accommodate the rest of your outfit under the piece.”

Are longer or shorter puffer jackets better?

We’ve included a variety of short puffer jackets and long puffer jackets to suit your needs. “Depending on the climate, long versus short makes a huge difference in the level of warmth you are,” says Deatherage. “I’m a big fan of an open long puffer that can accentuate the rest of an outfit. That being said, a short puffer is the more practical go-to option.”

What is the difference between a puffer jacket and a down jacket?

While some puffer jackets are made with down, others use a synthetic alternative, which creates a difference between down puffer jackets and non-down puffers. “My vote is always going to be premium down fill for puffer jackets,” says Volpe. “It's softer, higher quality, and just better overall 100 percent.” 

That said, not everyone is comfortable with wearing real down for sustainability reasons. “If you are opposed to down fill due to the fact that it’s made of geese and duck feathers, synthetic down fill such as polyester is a fair alternative,” she adds. “One upside to synthetic fill is it's water resistant versus down, which loses insulation when wet.” This makes materials like polyester fill better for machine-washing because it won’t break down like real down can. “Another plus is synthetic fill puffer jackets are more cost effective and affordable,” Volpe says.
