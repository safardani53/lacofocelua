Adam Spiegel, known professionally as Spike Jonze, is an American filmmaker, photographer, musician and actor whose work includes film, television, music videos and commercials. Jonze started his career as a teenager photographing BMX riders and snowboarders for Freestylin 'Magazine and Transworld Skateboarding, and he co-founded the youth culture magazine Dirt. Moving on to film making, he began shooting street skateboarding films, including the influential Video Days. Jonze co-founded the skateboarding company Girl Skateboards in 1993 with riders Rick Howard and Mike Carroll. Jonze's directing style made him a sought-after music video director for much of the 1990s, leading to collaborations with Sonic Youth, REM, Beastie Boys, Ween, Fatboy Slim, Daft Punk, Weezer, Björk, Kanye West and Arcade Fire.
