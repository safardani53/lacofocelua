A MOTHER has shared a one-of-a-kind soft play centre that’s perfect for a cheap day out with kids.

So if you live in the UK and are looking for reasonably-priced places to take your children this summer holiday, fear not, you’ve come to the right place.

Suemonai took to social media to document her day out at Dinotropolis - a children's amusement centre in Dartford. 

Dinotropolis is located inside Bluewater shopping centre, so you can take your little darlings and then head off round the shops or maybe even pop out a bite to eat.

Posting online, Suemonai shared her clip with the caption ‘Places to take kids in the UK.’

She said: “Another fun day out with the kids - so we visited Dinotropolis in Bluewater.

“First off we started off with putting our belongings away in the locks.

“Dinotropolis is a three-story soft play centre that is Dinosaur themed. How cool is that?

“The kids absolutely enjoyed themselves.

“I'll say it's more suited for children aged three plus, but there is plenty of soft play for babies as well.

“My daughter absolutely enjoyed herself with her cousin.

“They also had a cool sandpit area with Dinosaur bones laying around.

“This was a soft play area for the baby.”

Throughout the clip, Suemonai gave a close-up look at the huge soft play area, complete with Dinosaur animatronics, Raptor racing, ball pools, tunnel slides and secret walls.

There's even a facility to bring your own personal Dinosaurs to life.

Suemonai then shared how much entry costs, as she added: “0 to 18 months go for free.

“Child cost is £12, adult cost is £5.

“Free parking in Bluewater. Dartford Crossing charge if you're travelling by car.

“Overall we had a fun day out.” 

Suemonai’s TikTok clip, which was posted under the username @sueomanii, has clearly impressed many, as it has quickly amassed 41,800 views. 

But social media users were divided at Suemonai’s video - while some were eager to take a trip, others thought it was expensive for a child’s ticket. 

One person said: “It’s always too busy…” 

Another added: “I might have to give here another try.”

One mother noted: “Why is a child £12 and adult £5, I wanna play too.”
