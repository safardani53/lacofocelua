Mums reveal to Sun Online their horror stories of daughters hospitalised after getting ears pierced

April 13, 2024
blog

GETTING your ears pierced at Claire's Accessories is a rite of passage enjoyed by mums and daughters together across the UK.

But one mother's horror story about what can go wrong has sparked a wave of grim tales, which may make other parents think twice.

Earlier this week we reported how Lily Nesbitt, 7, was left screaming in agony and needing urgent hospital treatment after a piercing at the high street favourite.

And after publishing Lily's story, The Sun Online was inundated with mums giving similar accounts of infected ears after trips to Claire's.

Many said they thought their children’s earrings had disappeared – only to find they were embedded in their encrusted lobes.

They all claim to have diligently used Claire’s own brand healing solution – most of them splashing out three times as much to purchase the £15  3 Week Rapid After Care solution so that their kids’ ears would heal in time for school.

Every one of them, including those case studies listed below, has vowed never to step foot in Claire’s again.

In their response, Claire’s defended its reputation as Europe’s “Number One Ear Piercing Specialist” and said their piercing procedure and equipment were “designed to promote the safest and most hygienic piercing experience”.

Sophie Scott, eight

Debbie Scott, 45, says she was left "stressed and scared" after her daughter Sophie was placed under general anaesthetic to have an infected earring removed.

Her daughter Sophie had been "nagging" her to get her ears pierced and she gave in while they were shopping at the Arndale Centre in Manchester on January 2, choosing the "safer" option of gold earrings.

The piercing seemed to go well - but five weeks later her daughter woke up crying: "It's gone! It's gone!" The earring looked like it had fallen out but had actually become embedded in her ear.

Concerned Debbie, a kitchen assistant at Sophie's primary school, first took her daughter to the care centre at Halton Hospital.

There medics tried to remove the earring for an hour but Sophie became "too upset" to have it removed and was finally taken to the Alder Hey Hospital in Liverpool, where she was placed under general anaesthetic.

Mum Debbie is unsure what caused the infection, but is sceptical about the 3 Week Rapid Care Solution that she used daily.

"It's a waste of money," she said. "If it had worked like it should have done Sophie would never have got that infection."

She added: "Paying £15 for that solution seems mad when you could probably get a better result from salt water.

"Claire's really need to up their game and make sure their hygiene procedures and training are up to scratch. And they need to take another look at the solution."

Erin Moulton, six

Erin Moulton was left screaming in pain after her earring got "sucked into" her earlobe just a week after getting her ear pierced, her mum claims.

Julie Ryan from Shepherd's Bush, West London, says she took her daughter to Claire's in Westfield London on August 13 and returned a week later after swelling appeared in both ears.

Staff at the store told her to head to hospital, but at Hammersmith Hospital Julie was told they did not have the "correct instruments" to remove the earring.

Finally at Chelsea and Westminster Hospital the screaming child had to have numbing cream applied to her earlobe and was given gas and air for the doctor to push and yank the metal out.

Julie says she complained to Claire's head office but rejected an offer from the chain of a £42 refund plus £42 in-store compensation, describing it as "an insult".

She told the Sun Online: "My little girl was traumatised and was left in a lot of pain.

"This is completely irresponsible of Claire's and should not be happening to child of any age. I want parents to be fully aware of this terrible ordeal."

Sophia Gordon, six

Mum Natalia Zapior has told of a "disturbing" scene when she was made to endure the sight of her daughter screaming with pain.

Her daughter Sophia Gordon had her ears pierced at Claire's in Stafford in August 2017, so that her ears could be healed in time for the school term.

Natalia, 33, insists she used the £15 Rapid Care solution for six weeks before changing the earrings - taking twice the recommended precaution.

But after three months her right ear became infected when the butterfly "grew into her ear".

Doctors put Sophia, just five at the time, on antibiotics before the infection was aggravated and she was sent to A&E.

But they were unable to extract it and she was taken to the minor injuries clinic in Stafford the next day, where she was put under a local anaesthetic and underwent a 20 minute procedure to have the butterfly cut out.

"It was terrible, she was in serious pain and it was disturbing to watch," she told the Sun Online.

"There was loads of blood everywhere. I will never get her ears pierced again."

Sierra Glackin, nine

A nine-year-old autism sufferer who couldn't wait to get ears pierced was left "scarred for life" from an infection caused by a broken earring.

Sierra Glackin's mum Nichola said she believes the pearl earring used was "faulty" after claiming it broke off in her daughter's ear soon after the piercing in May last year.

But when she returned to the store to ask staff to remove it she claims to have been told that they "couldn't physically touch her" because of health and safety regulations.

She and a nurse friend were unable to remove the remaining piece and rushed the distressed nine-year-old to their local A&E, where like Lily Nesbit she was given gas and air while the earring was removed.

They were given a refund for the earrings but Nichola said her daughter was left deeply troubled by the experience.

She told the Sun Online: "As you can imagine my daughter was traumatised, she won't allow anyone to get near her ears now.

"She too had been excited at the prospect of wearing different earrings to match her peers.

"This is not an option and I believe the experience has scarred her for life."

Molly Coleman, eight

A schoolgirl's dream of getting her ears pierced quickly became a nightmare after she was left with bloody, gungy ears.

Molly Coleman's mum Jakki told the Sun Online how she shelled out £52 for her daughter's ear piercing on July 21 but that the stud fell out out on the very first night.

They drove the 23 miles from her home in Eltham, South London, to Bluewater Shopping Centre in Kent after the earring fell out twice, and finally staff fitted in different earrings.

After seven weeks of using the solution they changed the earring for a fresh pair, but it quickly became infected and she is currently unable to remove the infected studs.

The 35-year-old said they used the Rapid 3 Week After Care solution "religiously", saying: "They charge you £15 extra for it but it doesn't even seem to work. The cleaning solution is a joke."

She says she has phoned 111 and was told to continue cleaning the wound, but says it has started bleeding again and plans to take Molly to her GP.

"I can't get them undone as they are so tightly screwed on that she screams if I try as it hurts too much," she explained, adding: "I wish we'd never gone, we'll never use Claire's again."

Response from Claire's

'Our piercing procedure and all of our piercing instruments, supplies and earrings are designed to promote the safest and most hygienic piercing experience.

'The rapid after care lotion is dermatologist and paediatrician tested for the effective care of the pierced ear. Three weeks of use is an appropriate and approved length of time.

'The Rapid lotion is safe and allergy tested and its soothing formula is skin friendly.

'They added: "If undue pain / swelling / redness occurs at any time, seek medical advice immediately. Do not remove studs before the relevant medical advice."'
