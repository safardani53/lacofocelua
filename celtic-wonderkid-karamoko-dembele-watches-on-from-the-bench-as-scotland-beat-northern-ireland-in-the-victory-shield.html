THERE was to be no fairy tale for Karamoko Dembele as he had to watch from the bench as Scotland beat Northern Ireland in the Victory Shield.

The 13-year-old wonderkid was unable to contribute in the Under-16's opener, as the Celtic player is being fast-tracked by the Scottish FA.

Dembele already has the likes of Barcelona and Bayern Munich expressing an interest in him after a video recently emerged of the youngster's talents.

Even though he is currently making a name for himself in Scotland, he is qualified to play for England having been born there.

Ivory Coast have also expressed an interest for him to play for them, as it is his family's homeland.

All the attention was on him ahead of the game, but he failed to register a single minute of playing time, though he will have a chance to play in the remainder of the tournament, with Wales and the Republic of Ireland looming.

Scotland showed they don't need the speedy sensation, as Rangers midfielder Billy Gilmour starred in the victory collecting the man of the match award.

Goals from centre-back Taylor Wilson and Zac Butterworth who came on as a substitute clinched the win, as they look to stop Wales from winning a third straight Shield after England no longer provide a side for the competition.

Coach Brian McLaughin said after the game: "If we feel it's beneficial to play him, he plays.

"He'll become a better player just training with these boys.

"If we play someone or don't play someone, we do it with the best interests of the young player - or the young boy because that's all he is - in mind."

Asked about his inclusion in the squad at such a young age, he added: "I know the boy quite well so we made the decision it would be good for him, good for the other ones around him.

"We'll have other ones who probably feel they should be here, but I know them as well and I know as we're training here, they'll be training just as hard to make sure they get in the next squad."

Just two weeks ago, Dembele was thrown into Celtic's Under 20 side, where he featured in the last nine minutes of the match.

Current Hoops manager Brendan Rodgers is aware of the talents the teenager possesses and is eager to help him develop his skills while he is still boss.

Asked about him playing with people seven years older, he said: "I didn't know he was playing. Would I have sanctioned it? That's not the point. The point is he's played. You just have to be very careful."
