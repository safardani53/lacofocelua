HE is one of the wealthiest men in pop with a net worth of £260million.

But Ed Sheeran will be playing the exact opposite as a drug-addled down-and-out when he stars in Adam Deacon’s upcoming film Sumotherhood.

I’ve learned the superstar’s top secret role is as a homeless man struggling with a drug addiction in the action-comedy, which he shot in East London last year. 

The film also features Denise van Outen, Jennifer Saunders, Jaime Winstone, Peter Serafinowicz and Leomie Anderson, but it will be Ed’s transformation which is the most remarkable.

A movie insider said: “Ed plays this bloke who’s not got a home and looks like a crack addict. 

“It was filmed a while ago, but work on the movie is almost complete and the hope is it will be out this year.

Dua's song leak anger

DUA LIPA has been left scrambling for answers after suffering a series of high-profile leaks.

I can reveal that the Levitating singer and her team are concerned that tracks from her upcoming third album could be compromised after a song she worked on with Pharrell Williams appeared online this week.

While the track was an offcut from her record-breaking Future Nostalgia album, label chiefs are stumped as to how the song, along with several others in recent weeks, found its way into the public domain.

A source said: “Over the past month or so countless demos and rejected songs from Dua have been appearing online. 

“It’s annoying as they didn’t make the album for a reason but obviously the main concern is to know if information about her third album is next. 

“The stakes couldn’t be higher for the record after Future Nostalgia turned her into one of the biggest stars on the planet, so bosses are determined to plug the leak, and quickly. Future Nostalgia leaked a few days before it came out in 2020 and Dua was devastated. She has made it clear it must not happen again.”

Little is known about Dua’s upcoming record but she previously shared that she had a full vision for the album all lined up. She said: “It’s starting to feel good. It’s starting to take shape.

“I’m very excited about some of the new songs so it’s always exciting to look forward towards something. It has a vision. It has a name – for now.”

Glasto gig’s a Blondie moment 

BLONDIE have accidentally been confirmed for this year’s Glastonbury Festival, as drummer Clem Burke let slip the huge booking.

The group, who previously played the event in 1999 and 2014, will return to Worthy Farm in June.

Speaking on Foxy Radio, Clem said: “We’re also doing Isle of Wight Festival and some other festivals… I guess the full list of artists to be playing Glastonbury hasn’t really been announced yet.

“But we are going to be playing Glastonbury, so maybe you’ve got an exclusive there.” 

It’s a great booking for Glasto, given the group, fronted by Debbie Harry, have so many incredible hits including Heart Of Glass, Maria, Hanging On The Telephone, Call Me and Atomic.

Last month, Guns un’ Roses’ Duff McKagan also leaked their booking for the festival.
