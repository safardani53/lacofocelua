AN upmarket swingers’ club will throw open its doors next weekend for the first time since Covid lockdown – with a no-bonking rule.

The exclusive Eureka Club – where membership costs £400-a-year and couples pay £35 to attend parties – is holding a Saturday night bash.

But they’ll have marshals on hand to make sure wife-swappers aren’t overcome with lust and break social distancing rules.

Bosses have revealed they’ve built new cocktail and prosecco bars and new outside decking.

Accommodation in the grounds of the Kent club will also be open but Eureka’s swimming pool and playrooms will remain close.

Managers said that council bosses gave them the green light to reopen, revealing: “We have been in contact with our local authority and are pleased to announce we have permission to open our bistro, bars (including a new cocktail bar and a Prosecco bar) accommodation and our outside grounds for socials only.

“All inside facilities (excluding the toilet) and our swimming pool remain closed.

"Strict social distancing rules will be in place at all times.

"This includes safety marshals and toilet attendants for your safety.

“Numbers will be strictly limited and pre booking with a deposit is required.

“We look forward to welcoming you all back and building Eureka from strength to strength in the future.”

The club is set in 23 acres of parkland and has a 60ft swimming pool and a giant sauna with room for 25 people.

It has lodges and cabins dotted around the estate for exhausted revellers to stay over at up to £60-a-night.

The club is in the posh village of Fawkham in Kent, which has a 12th century church and is close to Brands Hatch race track.

Before lockdown Eureka hosted regular parties for “cougars and cubs”, fetish nights featuring whipping and special events for bisexuals, gays and cross-dressers.
