Ernest de Koven Leffingwell is an Arctic explorer, geologist, and Spanish-American War veteran. Between 1906 and 1914, Leffingwell spent nine summers and six winters on the Arctic coast of Alaska, making 31 sleigh and small boat trips. He created the first accurate map of much of Alaska's Arctic coast. He was the first to scientifically describe the permafrost and come up with theories of permafrost, which are largely believed to be correct. He accurately identified the oil potential of Alaska's North Slope.
