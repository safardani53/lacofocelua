Motorists face SEVEN MILE traffic jams on the London-bound M2 after lorry carrying hay goes up in fl

June 21, 2024
blog

A LORRY carrying bales of hay burst into flames on a motorway, causing seven-mile tailbacks.

Shocking video captured by passers-by showed the HGV engulfed in fire on the hard shoulder, with traffic building up behind it.

Emergency teams were called to the blaze on the London-bound M2 between Faversham and Sittingbourne in Kent at 10.15am on Friday.

Thoughtless motorists blocked the hard shoulder as fire crews fought their way through.

Six drivers will be prosecuted for obstructing a fire engine desperate to reach the huge blaze, traffic cops at Kent Police said.

The force's roads policing unit said on Twitter there was around seven miles of trapped traffic between junctions six and five of the M2, and heavy traffic on the A299 and A2.

A police spokesman said: "Officers are currently in attendance along with Kent Fire and Rescue Service and the carriageway has been closed while the incident is dealt with.

"No injuries are believed to have been reported.

"The incident is expected to take some time to deal with and so traffic is likely to be heavier in the area."

Kent Fire and Rescue Service said crews used hose reels to battle the flames and protect nearby corn fields.

They also used compressed air foam.

Highways England said motorists may want to use an alternative route as the clear-up continues.
