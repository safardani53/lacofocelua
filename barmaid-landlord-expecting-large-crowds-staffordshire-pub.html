Barmaid claims landlord was 'hiring staff for large crowds' at coronavirus pub where '400 people cra

March 13, 2024

A BARMAID who applied for a job at a pub where hundreds of customers were squeezed together “like sardines” today revealed the landlord hired extra staff as he was “expecting large crowds”.

Aisha Maynard, 21, claims she was warned by her new boss at the Crown & Anchor in Stone, Staffs, that the venue would be “rammed” most nights.

The mum-of-one said she was interviewed for the £10-an-hour bar job the day before pictures and video footage emerged showing the beer garden packed with around 400 customers on July 18.

The town centre pub has not reopened following the breach of government’s coronavirus rules and its owners have disappeared.

Aisha said the owners told her they were hiring “loads of staff” because they expected to be “heaving” throughout the summer, especially at weekends.

"RAMMED"

She told The Sun Online: “When I walked in for the interview, there were already six or seven girls waiting and there were more when I left.

“The boss said he expected the pub to be exceptionally busy and I’d have to be able to cope with large crowds. He said it would be rammed.

“I got the job and I was due to start this week but the pub closed after this now-notorious night and I haven’t heard anything since.

The owners haven’t contacted me to tell me what’s going on. I guess I need to start looking for a new job.”

Aisha said if she’d known the owners would act “irresponsibly” by allowing so many people to gather she would have turned the job down.

She added: “Bar owners have a duty to be responsible and protect people.”

Others in the town agree. Ray Bibby, 33, who is restaurant manager at a nearby venue in the town, said: “If you’re running a licensed premises, you just don‘t let that happen.

“In this pandemic climate, the onus is on all employers, especially those of us in the hospitality sector, to take our customers’ health super seriously.

“Pubs are opening too quickly and what happened at the Crown & Anchor shows the dangers.

"TOO QUICKLY"

“They had a doorman on duty that night so they could easily have kept a limit on the number of people on the premises.

“I drove past the pub on the night and people were queuing round the corner to get in around midnight. 

"They had to pay £5 to enter and nowhere else was open so the owners must have thought there was too much money to be made to turn them away."

A testing centre set up 400 metres from the pub was reportedly busy with a queue of people snaking along the street yesterday, but today only a handful of townsfolk wished to be tested for the virus.

One, who asked not to be named, said: “I phoned to arrange an appointment and was told they’d send me a home testing kit in the post so I got the impression they’re winding the test centre down.”

The pub released a statement last week, which read: "Due to the overwhelming amount of people visiting the Crown and Anchor on Saturday, we have decided to close this weekend to be able to review our risk assessment in order to deal with the difficulty that we faced with social distancing.

"By saying that we are grateful and appreciative of all our customers and want to make it a safe environment for you when we reopen next weekend. We will be posting our updated guidelines."

The Sun Online has contacted the pub for comment.
