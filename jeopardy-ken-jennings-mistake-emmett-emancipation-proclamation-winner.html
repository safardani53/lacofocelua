Jeopardy! fans furious after host Ken Jennings allows 2-day champ to win after most unacceptable'

February 19, 2024
blog

JEOPARDY! host Ken Jennings has allowed a final response that was deemed the most unacceptable-looking ever by furious fans on Monday.

Even the now-2-day champ who wrote it appeared unsure it would fly.

Jeopardy!'s shiny new season premiered on September 12th and alum Ken Jennings, 48, is hosting until January.

Before the now-airing Season 39, he and actress Mayim Bialik were officially announced as the permanent replacements for the late, great Alex Trebek.

Ken's first week as an official host - last week - made national news under the category of controversy when he allowed one player to correct his answer but cut off another from doing so during Wednesday's now-viral game.

With all eyes on the show's integrity, tonight's game saw a win with a Final Jeopardy response fans said they couldn't read whatsoever.

Returning champ Emmett Stanton - a freelance writer from Baltimore, Maryland - faced Suzanne Goss - an oncology advertising consultant from Brooklyn - and Jason Freeman - an event producer from Philadelphia, Pennsylvania.

Emmett won his first game on Friday when the vocal proponent of train travel derailed 5-day champ, Luigi de Guzman.

Scores going into Final Jeopardy were Suzanne at $8,600, Jason at $8,400, and Emmett at $8,000.

Neck and neck (and neck), the clue read: "The governor of Massachusetts wrote, it 'is a poor document, but a mighty act…wrong in its delay till January, but grand & sublime after all.'"

In the last round, players hand-write their responses and choose how much to risk.

Emmett revealed his response and wager first - he put it all on the line with a $8000 bet.

He appeared to have the correct response in mind - "Emancipation Proclamation" but his second word seemed to trail off completely.

He had crossed out his first go at it, and under "Emancipation" it simply looked barely there after the "Pr" - even he cringed with a smile as it was shown.

Ken replied to him: "What is the Emancipation... Proclamation" as the host himself seemed to take a moment to read it. "That is correct."

Jason and Suzanne did not have the correct response, but since the latter only bet $6000, she would have won on technicality had his answer not been accepted.

'YOU CAN'T BE SERIOUS'

On Reddit one wrote: "I'm not sure how the judges accepted the writing on Final Jeopardy today. The first half worked, but the second... whew."

Another tore: "There's no possible way to translate that last half into Proclamation, regardless of observed intent."

And a third: "I am surprised they accepted Emmett's FJ response. That was very illegible to my eyes. (not to downtalk the contestant, because panic would likely make me do the same chickenscratch)."

A fourth simply wrote: "Unbelievable lol."

Another said it looked like "'Imansupashun Proklomashin'"

A sixth brought up an infamous incident coming down to a similar handwritten response last season also in Final Jeopardy.

"I mean, in the aftermath of what happened with Sadie?" they wrote.

HANDWRITING HAVOK

Fan-favorite Sadie Goldberger's loss to Megan Wachpress back on June 20th came down to the judge's ruling that her otherwise correct Final Jeopardy response of "Harriet Tubman" was "incomplete".

Hundreds felt Sadie was "robbed" as she would have won had the show ruled in her favor - and were left upset calling it the season's "most painful loss".

The contestant has scored a comeback since she will be participating in the Second Chance Tournament in October.

On a recent episode of the "Inside Jeopardy!" podcast, Executive Producer Mike Davies was joined by producer and former "Clue Crew" member Sarah Foss - who hosts the weekly show with him.

They said Sadie's response was "unfinished" - not illegible - which is why it wasn't taken.

Producer Foss said with a sigh: "When it comes to Final Jeopardy, you have to finish your response."

"In Sadie's case, we did go back. We stopped the show, we went back and watched it repeatedly - we can see she went through each of the letters.

"She wrote a 'T-U-B-M-A' and we knew she had not begun to write an N.'"

The official Jeopardy! website states written responses to the Final Jeopardy! clue doesn't have to be spelled correctly to win, "but they must be phonetically correct and not add or subtract any extraneous sounds or syllables."

'CHANGE NEEDED'

Andy Saunders, who co-created the J-archive - which has chronicled every Jeopardy! game ever since 1984 on their free website, agreed after tonight that change is needed.

The expert wondered why the ever-evolving show hasn't switched to keypads for Final Jeopardy.

"As the show has said dozens of times now, the judges can view the contestants’ pens as they write their responses.

"They clearly saw Emmett write the letters of the correct response—how it looks at the end generally doesn’t factor into the judging. The judges’ call was thus correct."

"That being said, any issues at this point are 100% the show’s own doing.

"Just give the contestants a keyboard—we know the technology is there—and we completely eliminate any and all debates."

MEET THE HOSTS

Ken and Mayim began regularly filling in for the legendary Alex in 2021 after his tragic 2020 passing at age 80 and a whirlwind of Season 37 celebrity guest hosts.

They included Savannah Guthrie, fan-favorite LeVar Burton, Dr. Oz, Aaron Rodgers, Anderson Cooper, and many more who each scored a week or so at it.

The search also landed on former executive producer Mike Richards who scored the host gig from the inside before the shocking decision was "reversed" a week later when his misogynistic comments were dug up.
