KYLIE Jenner stepped out in Hollywood showing off her new bob hairstyle - but her sleek locks were overshadowed by her incredible figure.

The teenager - said to be back with on-off boyfriend Tyga - looked at least a decade older than her 18 years as she ran errands.

Unlike most youngsters, who'd opt for a tracksuit and trainers to wear out and about, Kylie chose a clinging black bodycon dress and a pair of towering heels.

Earlier this week, Kylie invited fans to see her having her hair cut in a Snapchat video.

But while some people find losing inches of hair a deeply emotional and cathartic experience, she simply captioned the clip, "Bye hair".

Kylie has a well documented love of changing her hairstyles, and has amassed a huge collection of wigs and hairpieces - and as far as she's concerned, no one has ever embraced fake hair like she has.

In April she caused outrage when she told Marie Claire: “I started wigs, and now everyone is wearing wigs.
“Kim [Kardashian] just used my wig guy last night... I just do whatever I want to do, and people will follow."

But it's easy to see that when it comes to copying, in the Kardashian-Jenner household it's a two-way street.

While big sister Kim, 35, might have turned to the 'trend-setting' teen for some hair advice, when it comes to style, Kylie is rinsing her older sibling for all she's worth.

As well as toning her figure to ridiculous hourglass proportions with the help of a corset and personal trainer, she's fully embraced Kim's love of all things clingy, revealing and low-cut.

The black dress Kylie wore could have been pinched straight from Kim's wardrobe, as could the pair of taupe heels.

Kylie also opted for the nude make-up tones favoured by Kim, and seemed to call upon all her concentration to keep her expression blank.

Perhaps she was thinking about her near-brush with plastic surgery hell.

Recently, in an interview with Kim in Allure magazine, Kylie admitted that she had gone a little overboard on lip fillers.

She said: "I got excited and felt like I needed to do a lot. And then you guys were like, 'Kylie, you need to chill.'

"And then I had to go back and have it fixed, and it was a crazy process.

"Thank God I didn't end up on 'Botched'."

The incident with the fillers won't have come as a surprise to Kylie after she predicted that 2016 would be the year of "realising stuff".

In a cringe-inducing video that she released online, Kylie sat next to a roaring fire, and carefully explains what she is hoping to achieve over the next twelve months – and it’s full on.

She drawls: “Hey guys I just wanted to answer a few questions about 2016.

“I feel like every year has a new energy and I feel like this year is really about the year of just ‘realising stuff’ and everyone around me, we’re all just realising things… 2016, looking good.”

Perhaps she's, like, realised that too-big lips don't, like, look good?
