N-Dubz announce new tour date after comeback gigs sell out in a HOUR

February 12, 2024
blog

N-DUBZ have announced another new tour date after their comeback gigs sold out in an HOUR last week.

The hip-hop trio revealed they are adding seven extra dates to their UK-wide tour for fans who missed out in the original sale.

N-Dubz, who split in 2011, thrilled fans when they announced last week they were due to reunite for a series of gigs.

Rapper Dappy (Costadinos Contostavlos), his cousin Tulisa Constostavlos and pal, Richard 'Fazer' Rawson, will reunite on stage.

After launching the Back To The Future tour, the trio sold out all the ten shows in under an HOUR following their launch online last Friday.

N-Dubz have now added extra dates at venues in Manchester, Nottingham, Birmingham, Leeds, Cardiff and London.

N-Dubz will now perform at the Newcastle Utilita Arena on November 7 before moving to the Glasgow OVO Arena on November 8.

Subsequent days see then visit Nottingham Motorpoint Arena, Leeds First Direct Arena and Birmingham Utilita.

The trio will then travel to Bournemouth International Centre and Cardiff Motorpoint Arena before their first day at London's The O2.

They will then head to Sheffield Utilita, Manchester's AO Arena for two days and Nottingham Motorpoint Arena.

It comes after N-Dubz released their first single in 11 years after they told of their comeback with the series of gigs.

"Fans have been calling for this for years but the timing has never been right," a source told The Sun last week.

"Charmer was initially due out May 13 but they decided to push things back to make sure everything about their comeback is perfect.

"Plans for the video were laid out like a military operation to keep everything under wraps."

Prior to releasing their album Uncle B in 2008 the group enjoyed critical acclaim, landing Best Newcomer at the MOBO Awards in 2007.

During their career, N Dubz enjoyed 2 Top 10 albums and 12 UK Top 40s.

They also bagged the top spot with a 2009 single called Number 1 alongside rapper Tinchy Stryder.
