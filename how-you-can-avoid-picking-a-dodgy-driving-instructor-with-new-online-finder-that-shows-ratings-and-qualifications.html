How YOU can avoid picking a dodgy driving instructor with new online finder that shows ratings and q

May 01, 2024
blog

PICKING a driving instructor is now easier than ever with a new finder service.

The updated DVSA tool can help you find a teacher that'll ensure you're top of the class.

The website search lists 26,000 approved instructors and you can filter out those who have agreed to display the grade awarded to them by regulators.

And you can also select to only show instructors who have signed up to the code of practice and committed to training each year.

Simply enter your postcode and draw up your filter and the names, numbers and, in some cases, driving school websites will be displayed alongside the rating and qualification data.

Only fully-qualified driving instructors - who have passed enhanced criminal record checks and had their ability to teach assessed to a set standard - are allowed to have their details shown on the service.

The moves comes after the DVSA hinted it would publish OFSTED-style driving instructor league tables if pass rates did not improve.

The worst tutors would be named and shamed in a bid to improve the standard of teaching and stop learners from being rushed through tests.

Gareth Llewellyn, DVSA chief executive, said: "DVSA’s priority is to help you through a lifetime of safe driving.

"It’s vital to choose the best approved driving instructor for you. They’ll help you learn the essential skills, knowledge and understanding you need to drive safely once you’ve passed your driving test.

"Making sure learner drivers have access to information that helps them choose the best instructor for their needs is part of our strategy to help you stay safe on Britain’s roads."
