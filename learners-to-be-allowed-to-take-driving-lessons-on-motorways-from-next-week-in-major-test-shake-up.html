Learners allowed to take driving lessons on motorways in major test shake-up

April 24, 2024
blog

LEARNER drivers are allowed to take lessons on motorways for the first time.

But students have to be accompanied by professional instructors under the new DVSA laws - so parents can't take their kids onto high-speed roads.

The new rule is part of the biggest ever shake-up in driver training and comes 60 years after the first motorway opened.

The changes are aimed at making learners more experienced before they're handed a licence.

Younger drivers are up to seven times more likely to be killed or seriously injured than drivers over 25.

Lessons will enable instructors to teach learners about how to join and leave motorways, how to overtake and when to switch lanes.

Driving at higher speeds will be invaluable, according to the DVSA, with training also focusing on smart motorway regulations for traffic signs and breakdowns.

Transport Minister, Jesse Norman, said: “Allowing learners to drive on motorways, with the support of an experienced driving instructor, will help them to develop a practical understanding of how to use motorways safely, and it will prepare them to drive on them alone.”

But despite the obvious benefits for new drivers, a recent study found that more than half of British motorists are against the driving test change.

A 56 per cent majority said it would be "dangerous" to let inexperienced drivers on motorways.

The changes only apply to England, Wales and Scotland and motorways won't form part of the actual exam just yet.

Only approved driving instructors with dual controlled cars will be allowed to give motorway lessons - so if you're being taught by parents you won't be allowed to test out the fast lane.

the road laws you need to know

Gareth Llewellyn, DVSA chief executive, said: "By allowing learners to have lessons on motorways, we are making sure learners get the skills and experience they need to drive on fast, busy roads.”

The motorway law is the latest change to the driving test following the introduction of new manoeuvres and independent driving last year.

They require candidates to follow a sat nav for up to 20 minutes and parallel park, and came into force on December 4.
